Help get funding for Computer Science education!

Here's the skinny.

The Computer Science Education Coalition wants Congress to provide $250 million for K-12 computer science education this fiscal year.

We, as a country, aren't educating enough CS grads to fill necessary jobs.

There are over 500,000 computing jobs currently unfilled in the U.S., and only 42,969 CS students graduated from U.S. universities into the workforce last year.

Between 2016 and 2020, the US projects there will be 960,000 job openings and only 344,000 graduates to fill them.

You can help.

It's estimated that an initial $250 million in federal funds could support as many as 52,500 classrooms, which could potentially reach 3.6 million students across the U.S. in the coming year.
